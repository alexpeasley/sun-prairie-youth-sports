‚öæ Baseball & Softball (SPYBAS)

The primary organization for youth ball. It is designed to develop skills from the ground up, starting with T-ball and moving into competitive player-pitch leagues.

  • Rec League: Focused on participation and fun for 4K-8th grade. Registration runs January through mid-March.
  • Travel Teams (Starz): A higher level of competition involving weekend tournaments across the state. Tryouts for the following summer occur in late July/August.
  • Links: Rec Registration | Travel Info

üèÉ Track & Field (Sun Prairie Bolts)

A competitive summer track program for athletes ages 7-18. This is not just a running club; it is a sanctioned AAU/USATF club that prepares athletes for state and national meets.

  • Events: Includes Sprints, Distance, Hurdles, Long Jump, Shot Put, and High Jump.
  • Schedule: Practices begin in May; meets run June through July.
  • Links: Bolts Registration Page

‚öΩ Soccer (Sun Prairie Soccer Club)

SPSC offers both recreational and "Academy" style soccer. Academy soccer focuses on technical footwork and higher-level play compared to standard recreational leagues.

  • Levels: U5 through U19. Includes "In-House" leagues for younger kids and "State" leagues for older competitive players.
  • Season: Dual season (Fall and Spring). Spring registration usually closes in late February.
  • Links: SPSC Registration

üèä Summer Swim (Sun Prairie Piranhas)

The Piranhas are a community swim team that competes in the Tri-County league. It is a great way for kids to experience competitive swimming in a low-pressure, fun environment at the local outdoor pool.

  • Requirement: Kids must be able to swim one length (25m) of the pool without assistance or floatation.
  • Links: Piranhas Registration

üèà SP East Youth Football (Cardinals)

Full-contact tackle football for grades 4-8. This program is the direct feeder for Sun Prairie East High School, ensuring kids learn the specific offensive and defensive systems they will use in high school.

  • Timeline: Registration opens April 1st. Equipment handout is usually in July.
  • Links: East Football Registration

üèà SP West Youth Football (Wolves)

Full-contact tackle football for grades 4-8 for families in the Sun Prairie West boundary. Focuses on "Wolfpack" culture and technical safety in tackling.

  • Timeline: Registration opens April 1st. Season begins with "Heat Week" in early August.
  • Links: West Football Registration

ü§º Wrestling (Community & Private)

Wrestling offers some of the best cross-training for other sports. Sun Prairie has a robust community club and a year-round private facility.

  • SP Youth Wrestling: The K-8 community club. Includes weekly practices and optional weekend tournaments. Perfect for all skill levels. Club Site
  • JJ's Boxing & Wrestling: Provides high-level technical wrestling instruction and boxing. Known for intensive training and technical development. JJ's Site

üèÄ Basketball (SYBA)

The Sun Prairie Youth Basketball Association is the primary travel/feeder program for grades 3-8. This is a competitive league that requires evaluations for team placement.

  • Tryouts: Mandatory tryouts occur in September. Registration opens in August.
  • Links: SYBA Registration Info

üèí Hockey (Sun Prairie Groundhogs)

Sun Prairie Youth Hockey (SPYHA) is one of the most active associations in the region, operating out of the Sun Prairie Ice Arena.

  • Mini-Mites: A "Learn to Play" program for kids as young as 4.
  • Competitive: Squirts, PeeWees, and Bantams play full-season travel schedules.
  • Links: Hockey Registration
